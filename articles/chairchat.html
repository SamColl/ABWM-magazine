Chat from the Chair

Well it’s been a little while since we’ve had a Chairman’s Chat!

Our last hard copy magazine was the joint December/January issue and we’ve had ‘a few months off’ to get our online version ready for this April re-launch.

I say ‘a few months off’ with my tongue very firmly in my cheek, as it has been a steep learning curve and taken a lot of woman hours to get us to this point – a particular shout out to Lesley Whittle, who has taken the lead on this for us and to Sam Roberts, our Tech Wonder Woman for making it all happen. Also, sincere thanks to Philippa Matthews, who’s brought a designer’s eye to our machinations and to Alia Dada, our new Editor (talk about a baptism of fire Alia!).



The online magazine has a new identity too, visually blending together the UK and KL and is optimally viewed on an I iPad/tablet – preferably with a cup of coffee and optional biscuit in hand. We expect that it will reach a wider audience than our printed magazine, as it will be available to both members and non-members through the our ABWM.com.my website. For those who are exploring the prospect of a move to Kuala Lumpur, it will give them a taste of the Charity, Fun and Support offered by the ABWM; for those who have moved on to pastures new, it will help keep them in touch.



For our advertisers, it will also mean greater exposure. Clicking on their ads will take you directly to their website, so that you can read more about their businesses and what they have to offer you. Many advertisers have been saying to us for some time that they were moving their advertising to online media, so we hope that we will also benefit from more advertisers looking at ABWM as a viable option for them.

I hope that when you read this first new format issue that it will feel reassuringly familiar but refreshed. All your old favourites are still here: Five Minutes Peace, Film Reviews, coverage of our social events, updates on local charities and information on upcoming events.



We view it very much as a work in progress though, so please feel free to contact Alia with your feedback (abwmeditor@gmail.com). We’d particularly like to hear from people who would like to contribute articles in the future: read this month’s contribution from Mel Bolland, on her experience of learning to drive the Malaysian way – something which a number of us are having to contemplate since the change to the law that means having a UK driving licence is not enough. Tell us about that great trip, the wonderful meal, the funny experiences….Malaysia lah!

Enjoy!
