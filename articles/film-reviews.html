Film Reviews

Title: Mary Poppins Returns
Director: Robert Marshall
Starring: Emily Blunt, Lin-Manuel Miranda, Ben Wishaw, Julie Walters, Dick Van dyke



Widowed Michael Banks, is trying hard to keep things together for the sake of his children and his sister. On threat of his house being repossessed, (by the bank he works for) he starts a desperate search for a shares certificate his late father left.

Meanwhile, the children are amazed when greeted by a lady, who appears to be flying with an umbrella! Immediately she saves them from imminent danger and tells them she is here to clean them up and look after them, like she did their dad and auntie. Magical trips into days gone by see Mary Poppins and her literal bag of tricks in action!

On finding the document and it being 1930 they know that unless they can turn back time they won’t make it! In true Disney style, not only do they stop and rewind time, they save the house and their family. Mary Poppins’ work is now complete!

Great film for all ages, catchy songs and laughable antics for all.



Film Fact: Dick Van Dyke appears in the film, only having one role this time, not two like the original!
Length: 130 minutes
Release Date: 23rd December
Rating: PG

Title: Ocean's 8
Director:Gary Ross
Starring: Sandra Bullock, Cate Blanchett, Anne Hathaway, Helena Bonham Carter, Rhianna



Debbie Ocean, sister of the late Danny, has been hatching an almighty heist whilst serving a prison sentence. On release, she gets the ‘old’ team together, amongst them a phenomenal pick pocket, a costume jeweler and a deluded almost bankrupt designer. She sets into motion her plan to steal a 150 million dollar Cartier necklace. An elaborate party sees eight extremely talented ladies with a plan so good, it includes a substitute necklace being made in the time it takes a model to be sick! They have managed to orchestrate their movements to avoid detection by the security cameras, staff on the job on the inside, and time to make the initial loss of the necklace look like an accident, when they find it in a fountain. It’s as if the sick model had dropped it there by accident! Genius plan, great film and cast and a story that follows the plots of the Ocean 7 series!



Film Fact: Matt Damon & Carl Reiner where originally set to reappear but they cut the scenes to better fit the story.
Length: 110 minutes
Release Date: 8th June 2018
Rating: 15

Title: Bohemian Rhapsody
Director: Brian Singer
Starring: Rami Malek, Lucy Boynton, Gwilym Lee, Ben Hardy



Following the turbulent lives of Queen; this fantastic biographical account of their success, will have any fan or admirer, laughing, toe tapping and maybe even singing through the film.

Freddie secures lead vocals with an impromptu solo, outside a gig. Queen, work hard gigging, and even sell their touring van to produce their solo album. After recording Bohemian Rhapsody they walk out of EMI ecords as they refuse to play the six minute long track. Unperturbed, they release the record and make another album and success is theirs for the taking, but it comes with issue. Freddie is grappling with his sexuality and has to break of his engagement with Mary – his one and only true love. Swayed by the press and solo music career, Freddie works alone, while the band are trying to reunite to partake in Band Aid. On finding out his lover and manager has withheld this information, Mary makes him see what he’s missing, reconciles with the band for the ultimate show of shows.



Film Fact: Brian May & Roger Taylor had creative rights over the film
Length: 138 minutes
Release Date: 2nd November 2018
Rating: 18

By Rachael Garnett
