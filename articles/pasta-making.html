Tortelli Workshop at Michelangelo’s

Have dough. Make pasta. A day of rolling, folding, laughing and eating at the tortelli workshop held at Michelangelo's

Tortelli are super-sized tortellini, which we learned how to make, with varying degrees of success. We were perhaps better at eating them, along with salads, bruschetta and dessert, not to mention a glass of wine.


Michelangelo’s is an authentic Italian restaurant in Solaris Mont Kiara, which is just down the road from Publika. It was suggested as a venue for a Pasta Workshop after a successful series of workshops they ran for Gardens International School. Thank you Ying (Hacker’s mum) for the introduction!

After a lemon tea boost, we gathered around a “Salmon and Crab” table or a “Spinach and Ricotta” table to use shiny pasta machines to roll out the pre-prepared dough into successively thinner and thinner layers. More than one person let on that they actually owned one of these machines, but had only used it once after receiving it as a wedding present. There are pasta machines in the back of cupboards that have been shipped to multiple locations around the world and have enough air miles to qualify for their own gold card.

A rolling zigzag cutter gave the pretty scalloped edge, and then the tricky bit. Folding the squares into triangles around the filling and then somehow making them look professional! Harder than it looks. (See the photos of the Good, the Bad and the Aesthetically Challenged.)

After being served a restorative glass of wine, we ate the fruits of our labours, which were delicious. Michelangelo’s were extremely well-organised and the staff were refreshingly efficient. Many thanks to chef Massimo (usually to be found at their Pavilion branch), sous chef Mark and manager Darshan, as well as to Dilys Chaim who made it all possible. Thanks also to Asian Tigers for providing our raffle wine, which was won by Bethan Cole.

There were many compliments made about the event, and we had a waiting list. If we run another of these workshops in the autumn, I recommend you come along, as it was a good laugh and a fun lunch.

Micelangelo’s Restaurant
Soho KL, Lot J-OC-03 & 04,
2 Jalan Solaris
Solaris Mont Kiara, 50480
